01
Buy LAMP shares on exchange

You place a buy order for ~$10,000 of LAMP through any brokerage — Schwab, Fidelity, Interactive Brokers, Robinhood. LAMP trades on a national securities exchange like any other ETF.

At a NAV of $61.87 per share, $10,000 buys you approximately 161 shares.

No crypto wallet. No prediction market account. No KYC beyond your existing brokerage. You're buying a regulated ETF share.

What you own

02
Fund enters Total Return Swap

The LAMP fund does not buy prediction market contracts directly. Instead, it enters into a Total Return Swap (TRS) with institutional swap counterparties.

The TRS gives the fund synthetic exposure to the 17-contract reference basket. The counterparties handle all on-chain execution, custody, and settlement on Polymarket's CLOB and Kalshi's order book.

The fund holds cash collateral. The swap counterparties bear the execution risk. Your shares are backed by a traditional finance instrument — not crypto tokens.

04
Daily NAV marking

Every trading day, the fund calculates a new Net Asset Value from composite mid prices across Polymarket and Kalshi.

Your 161 shares reflect the weighted average price of all 17 contracts. When prediction markets move — a candidate surges, a policy shifts, a conflict escalates — your NAV moves with it.

Example

Democrats are currently priced at 86¢ to win the House in 2026. If that price rises to 92¢ overnight, the House Control position (5.4% of your basket) gains ~7%. On your $540 exposure to that contract, that's roughly +$38 to your NAV.

05
Contracts resolve

Each prediction market contract is binary. When the event occurs (or doesn't), the contract pays out:

  • YES = $1.00 — the event happened
  • NO = $0.00 — the event did not happen

Resolution is determined by a triple-source oracle system:

  • Elections: AP, Fox News, NBC must agree
  • Macro/Policy: Fed.gov, official government sources
  • Geopolitical: Major wire services + government confirmation
Example — your $540 in House Control

You have exposure at 86¢ (Democrats favored). If Democrats win the House in November 2026, the contract resolves at $1.00. Your $540 exposure becomes worth $628 — a gain of $88 (16.3% return on that position).

If Republicans win, the contract resolves at $0.00 on the Democrat side. That $540 goes to zero. But the Republican side of other basket positions may offset.

06
Monthly rebalance

At the end of each month, the fund rebalances the basket:

  • Resolved contracts are removed and replaced with new high-liquidity markets
  • Weights are recalculated based on current open interest and liquidity depth
  • New contracts may be added as new prediction markets gain volume

This keeps the basket current, liquid, and representative of the highest-conviction crowd intelligence available.

07
Redemption

You have two ways to exit your position:

  • Secondary market: Sell your LAMP shares on the exchange at any time during market hours, just like any ETF. Liquidity depends on market maker spreads.
  • NAV redemption: Redeem directly at NAV through quarterly redemption windows with 30-day advance notice. This matches the real liquidity profile of the underlying prediction market contracts.
